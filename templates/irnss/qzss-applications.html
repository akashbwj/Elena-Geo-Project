Applications of QZSS

In Japan, mobile penetration rate has reached 92.4% in 2010 and almost 50% of mobile terminals have GPS functions, so positioning information using satellites is imperative to peoples' lives.

The successful launch of QZSS, MICHIBIKI (which means "Guidance" in Japanese) has helped to make our society more secure and comfortable as it enhances the accuracy of positioning information and makes it ubiquitous.

Japan has established technology to utilize GPS signals for navigation systems, and the high-accuracy QZSS positioning system promises to expand the possibilities in a variety of fields.

Examples of QZSS Applications:

  1. Environment Conservation:Combination of LBS (Location Based Services) and value-added satellite remote sensing information provides advanced telematics, which solves traffic problems and reduces exhaust fumes and energy consumption.
  2. Realizing Secure Society:Sending location, time, images, etc. to Operations Center in case of an accident.
  3. Agriculture Business:Solutions to issues like deviation of farm field soil component and difficulties of optium harvesting utilizing GNSS and Satellite Remote Sensing.
  4. Personal Services:Utilizing AR (Augmented Reality) technology, old-time CG landscape, historical photos, etc. could be displayed.
